ICRA 2026 Workshop

Industry-Ready Robotics

Optimal Robots, Workcells, Planning, and Deployment in Flexible Manufacturing

Workshop Content

Modern Manufacturing

Modern manufacturing is undergoing rapid transformation driven by increasing product variability, small batch sizes, and rising demands for speed and adaptability. Automation systems must meet a challenging set of requirements: high-mix/low-volume (HMLV) production, short programming and setup times, high throughput, and energy efficiency. This workshop focuses on industry-ready robotic solutions that address these demands through optimized workcell design, intelligent scheduling, and efficient deployment strategies.

Technological Advances

The performance of robotic systems in manufacturing environments is influenced by numerous interdependent factors, including robot kinematics, component placement, task definition, task allocation, scheduling, and motion planning. Optimizing all these components jointly is crucial to achieving high system performance while minimizing cycle time, energy consumption, and downtime. At the same time, technological advances are opening up new opportunities for robotic systems. These include increased computational resources for offline and online planning, modular, sensor-rich robots with real-time processing capabilities, tight integration with factory-level scheduling, and the use of AI-driven algorithms for perception, decision-making, and adaptation.

Real-World Applications

This workshop brings together researchers, industry experts, and practitioners to explore the latest developments in flexible, intelligent, and efficient robotic manufacturing. Emphasis is placed on real-world applicability, from system design to deployment. Topics include optimal workcell design, modular robots, constrained task and motion planning, and AI-enabled industrial workflows.

Key Topics

In the context of industrial HMLV manufacturing and re-manufacturing, the key topics include

System design

Design and optimization of modular robot systems, robot work cells, multi-robot work cells, and production lines

Planning

Motion planning with kinematic, dynamic, and collision constraints; task planning, allocation, and scheduling

Advancements

AI-driven advancements in perception, planning, decision-making, and adaptation

Deployment

Industrial workflows, data structures, and data management
